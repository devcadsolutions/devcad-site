# The Coffee Pup

The Coffee Pup is a responsive coffee ordering web app built around a warm cafe-style product experience. It gives customers a simple way to explore drinks and pastries, customize an order, review their cart, and move through checkout from either desktop or mobile.

## Background

The project presents a digital storefront for a cafe brand with a polished ordering flow. The experience combines a product-focused home screen, category-based menu browsing, item customization, checkout details, and a community area for announcements and customer reviews.

The visual direction leans into a handcrafted coffee identity: warm brown accents, soft cards, serif display headings, product photography, and large touch-friendly controls. The interface is designed to feel more like a modern mobile ordering app than a traditional static website.

## Role

Product UI, responsive frontend implementation, and end-to-end ordering flow design for a Web Apps & Sites portfolio project.

## Features

- Responsive home experience with featured products and cafe announcements
- Category-based menu browsing for coffee, signature drinks, matcha, non-coffee drinks, bottles, toasts, and pastries
- Product cards with pricing, bestseller labels, and add-to-order actions
- Drink customization flow with size, quantity, and order actions
- Cart and checkout screens with customer details, delivery method, notes, and payment options
- Order confirmation and order status screens
- Account/login screen for customer identity
- Community section with announcements and customer reviews
- Desktop and mobile layouts designed from the same product experience

## Stack

- React

- Cloudflare Workers
- Responsive web app UI

## Case Study

### Goal

The goal was to create a cafe ordering experience that feels quick, friendly, and product-led. Instead of presenting the cafe as a simple brochure site, the app brings the customer directly into the buying flow: browse the menu, customize a drink, add items to cart, and complete checkout.

### Solution

The Coffee Pup uses an app-style layout with persistent navigation, strong product photography, and clear ordering actions. The home screen introduces the brand with the headline "Your Morning Ritual, Handcrafted." and gives customers two immediate paths: build a drink or view the menu.

The menu is organized by category so customers can move between product groups quickly. Each item includes a product image, short description, price, and action button. The customization screen turns product selection into a guided flow, with quantity controls and size options before the customer adds the item to the cart or proceeds to order.

Checkout is intentionally straightforward. It asks for customer details, contact number, delivery method, notes, and payment method, then keeps the total and confirm action visible at the bottom of the screen. Supporting screens such as account, order confirmation, and community reviews round out the app so it feels like a complete customer experience rather than only a menu prototype.

### Product Highlights

- App-like navigation keeps Home, Orders, Menu, Community, and Account within easy reach
- The hero section communicates the brand mood immediately with a handcrafted cafe message
- Product categories make a large menu easier to scan
- Customization screens support real ordering decisions such as quantity and size
- Checkout includes local payment and delivery options such as GCash, Maya, Lalamove, Grab, and MoveIt
- Community content gives the app a place for announcements, schedules, and customer trust signals
- The mobile version keeps ordering actions large and reachable for touch use

### Technical Notes

The app is delivered as a React single-page web app and deployed through a Cloudflare Workers URL. The interface uses reusable product, navigation, checkout, and community screens, with iconography from lucide-react and a utility-driven styling approach consistent with Tailwind CSS.

The screenshots show the same experience adapted across desktop and mobile, including wide product grids on larger screens and more compact card-based layouts on mobile. The structure supports a portfolio-friendly product story because it demonstrates both visual polish and practical ordering workflows.

### Outcome

The finished project works as a complete cafe ordering concept: customers can discover products, browse categories, configure an item, manage checkout details, and view supporting community information. For a portfolio, it shows responsive UI design, e-commerce flow thinking, product detail handling, and a warm branded visual system.
